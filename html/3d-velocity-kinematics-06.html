Inverting the Jacobian Matrix (3D)

The Jacobian matrix can be inverted so long it is

  • square,
  • and not singular.

(see here)

The inverse Jacobian allows us to determine the joint velocity given the end-effector velocity.

Resolved Rate Motion Control

This technique is same as for the 2D situation except the joint velocity is computed as \(\mathbf{\dot{q}}=\mathbf{J}(\mathbf{q}_k)^{-1}\nu\)
